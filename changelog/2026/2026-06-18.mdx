## Improvements

🔗 You can now control [public onboarding links](/topics/onboarding/#links) separately for creating and updating accounts. If you already have public links enabled, both are on by default — adjust them anytime in your Dashboard under **Onboarding** > **Public Links**.

🔄 All accounts closed before April 2, 2026 now use the [2026 closure reason codes](/topics/accounts/closure/#closure-reason-codes). Your whole portfolio is consistent, so you can run data analysis across your full history.

🔄 All capital deposits canceled before April 2, 2026 now use the [2026 cancelation reason codes](/topics/capital-deposits/#cancelation-reason-codes). As with closures, your whole portfolio is consistent, so you can run data analysis across your full history.

📩 You can now see how your onboarding and Know Your Customer (KYC) email notifications are configured, from your Dashboard under **Settings** > **Notifications**. No need to contact Swan just to check your setup. The following notifications are now visible:

- **Your account terms and conditions** — always on, and can't be deactivated or edited for compliance reasons.
- **First transfer** — view your current setup; contact Swan to change it.
- **Request supporting documents** — view your current setup; contact Swan to change it.
- **Request supporting documents reminders** — view your current setup; contact Swan to change it.

🔍 You can now filter [virtual IBANs](/topics/accounts/ibans/#virtual) by status — `Enabled` or `Canceled` — with the `status` filter on [`virtualIbanEntries`](https://api-reference.swan.io/objects/virtual-ibanentry-connection/) in the [`account`](https://api-reference.swan.io/queries/account/) query. This makes it easy to surface only active IBANs, even when an account has many of them.

📋 You can now create a [capital deposit](/topics/capital-deposits/) case for companies whose legal name is longer than 100 characters. The limit on the `companyName`, `accountName`, and `name` fields rises from 100 to 255 characters, matching standard French registry formats. No changes needed on your end.

🔄 The conditions for an account to move from `Closing` to `Closed` status are changing in two ways:

- The window for the last outgoing [SEPA Credit Transfer](/topics/payments/credit-transfers/sepa/) grows from 5 to 30 days.
- A new condition applies: no incoming or outgoing transaction can be in `Upcoming` or `Pending` status, for any payment type.

All other conditions stay the same, so accounts may sit in `Closing` status longer than before.

🔄 Incoming transfers to a `Closing` account are now accepted when the booked balance is negative. This helps settle outstanding debts before the account fully closes. Transfers to `Closing` accounts with a zero or positive balance are still returned, as before.

## API updates

### Upcoming breaking changes

⚠️ The `DormantAccount` closure reason is **deprecated**. [`Inactivity`](https://api-reference.swan.io/enums/close-account-reason-type/) replaces it, with the description: "Prolonged inactivity according to local regulatory definitions or internal policy." All accounts that previously used `DormantAccount` are now migrated to `Inactivity`.

If your integration filters, displays, or processes closure reasons by code, update it to handle `Inactivity` and remove any reference to `DormantAccount`. See the [updated closure reason codes](/topics/accounts/closure/#closure-reason-codes) and [deprecated reason codes](/topics/accounts/closure/#deprecated-reasons) for the full mapping.
