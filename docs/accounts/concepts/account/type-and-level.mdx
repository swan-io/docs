# Account type and level

Swan accounts are defined by a payment **level** and payment **account type**.
There are two possible values for each:

- [`PaymentLevel`](https://api-reference.swan.io/enums/payment-level) â†’ `Limited` and `Unlimited`
- [`PaymentAccountType`](https://api-reference.swan.io/enums/payment-account-type/) â†’ `EMoney` and `PaymentService`

Accounts start with the level `Limited` and the account type `EMoney`.
The level changes from `Limited` to `Unlimited` when the [account holder verification status](/accounts/concepts/account-holders/verification#verification-process-statuses) changes to `Verified`.
The account type changes from `EMoney` to `PaymentService` when the [account's main IBAN](/accounts/concepts/ibans/main) is available to the <Term id="account-holder">account holder</Term>.
Accounts with the level `Unlimited` and the account type `PaymentService` are fully functional accounts.

## Capabilities {#capabilities}

Swan accounts have different capabilities determined by the combination of these values.

| Level + Type | Capabilities |
| --- | --- |
| `Limited` + `EMoney` | <Yes /> The account can be used for certain actions, such as inviting account members and adding virtual cards.<br /><br /><No /> Account holder verification isn't complete.<br /><No /> The account's main IBAN isn't displayed.<br /><No /> Payments can't be made from the account. |
| `Limited` + `PaymentService` | <Yes /> The account's main IBAN is displayed.<br /><Yes /> Money can be added to the account.<br /><br /><No /> Account holder verification isn't complete.<br /><No /> Payments can't be made from the account.<br /><No /> Incoming SEPA Credit Transfers exceeding â‚¬50 are automatically rejected.<br /><No /> Maximum cumulative balance is â‚¬150.<br /><No /> Incoming International Credit Transfers are automatically rejected regardless of amount. |
| `Unlimited` + `PaymentService` | <Yes /> Account holder verification status is `Verified`.<br /><Yes /> Payments can be made from the account.<br /><Yes /> The account's main IBAN is displayed.<br /><Yes /> All accounts functions are possible. |

:::info Restrictions on incoming transfers for limited accounts

`Limited` accounts are subject to the following restrictions:
- Each incoming SEPA Credit Transfer must be **â‚¬50 or less**.
- A `Limited` account can't receive more than â‚¬150 in SEPA Credit Transfers over its lifetime.

The â‚¬150 limit applies to the cumulative sum received, not the current account balance.
Swan fees don't count toward this limit. For example, if a user receives â‚¬150 and is charged a â‚¬2 Swan fee, their balance will be â‚¬148. 
Even though the fee reduced their balance, the â‚¬150 received counts towards their limit. 
Swan will automatically reject any further transfers sent to the account.

These restrictions apply to both SEPA Credit Transfers (instant and regular).
Incoming International Credit Transfers are automatically rejected regardless of amount.

Accounts are in `Limited` status before they are verified. 
These restrictions primarily impact first transfers during the account holder verification process.
