# Capital deposit statuses

A capital deposit tracks three interlocking state machines: the **case**, each **shareholder**, and each **document**. They advance together, and the case status reflects where the whole deposit stands.

## The case is the master clock {#case}

The [`CapitalDepositCase`](https://api-reference.swan.io/objects/capital-deposit-case) status paces the whole deposit. It moves from initial requirements through Swan's review and the notary steps to `Completed`, or to `Canceled` at any point. The case can't pass a step until the shareholders and documents it depends on are ready.

For the full state machine and every value, see [case statuses](/accounts/reference/capital-deposits#case-statuses) and the [cancelation reason codes](/accounts/reference/capital-deposits#cancelation-reason-codes) attached to a canceled case.

## Shareholders advance alongside it {#shareholders}

Each [shareholder](/accounts/concepts/capital-deposits/shareholders) carries their own status reflecting how far they've onboarded and transferred funds: from `PendingOnboarding`, through verification and transfer, to `CapitalFundsWiredToNotary`. The case only completes once every shareholder reaches that final state.

For the full state machine and every value, see [shareholder statuses](/accounts/reference/capital-deposits#shareholders-statuses).

## Documents gate progress {#documents}

Each required document moves from `Pending` to `Uploaded`, then to `Validated` or `Refused`. A `Refused` document must be re-uploaded before the case can move forward.

For the full state machine and every value, see [document statuses](/accounts/reference/capital-deposits#documents-statuses).

## How statuses cascade {#cascade}

The three models aren't independent. Beyond the forward gating described above, the dependency also runs in reverse: when a `CapitalDepositCase` is `Canceled`, every shareholder status flips to `CapitalDepositCanceled`, and any temporary account already opened for a shareholder is closed automatically.
