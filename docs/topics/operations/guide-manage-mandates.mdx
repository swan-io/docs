# Manage SEPA Direct Debit mandates

This guide helps you manage SEPA Direct Debit mandates from the Partner Dashboard, including reviewing mandates, troubleshooting rejections, and knowing when to escalate to your development team or Swan Support.

:::info Key takeaways
- Review received mandates and check their status directly from your Dashboard.
- Updating or stopping a B2B mandate requires your development (dev) team. You can't do this in the Dashboard.
- Swan checks mandate statuses and available balances at **20:00 CET/CEST** on the day the payment is due.
- The most common cause of rejected direct debits is incomplete B2B consents.
- Refund rights differ by scheme: SDD Core allows refunds up to 8 calendar weeks; SDD B2B has no right to refunds.
:::

## Understand direct debit mandates

When a company (creditor) needs to take money from your user's account; for subscriptions, rent, or utility payments, they use a **direct debit**. Your user (debtor) authorizes this by signing a **mandate**.
Swan supports two SDD schemes:

| Scheme | Who can use it | Registration required? | Refund rights |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Core** | Individuals and companies | No. This is handled automatically by the creditor. | Up to 8 calendar weeks |
| **B2B** | Companies only | **Yes.** The user must register a mandate and consent. | Not possible |

For a full explanation of how SEPA Direct Debit works, including mandate types, statuses, and API integration, refer to the [SEPA Direct Debit overview](https://docs.swan.io/topics/payments/direct-debit/).

| Reason | What's happening | Where to look |

## Send a B2B mandate registration link

To send a B2B mandate registration link from your Dashboard or add a mandate with the API, refer to the guide to [add a received direct debit mandate](https://docs.swan.io/topics/payments/direct-debit/guide-add-mandate/). ## Review received mandates from your Dashboard

To check existing mandates:

1. Go to **Dashboard** > **Data** > **Accounts**.
1. Select the account and open the **Received direct debit mandates** tab.
1. Review mandate details: creditor and debtor information, status, signature date, and last execution date.

## Update a B2B received mandate

Updating a B2B received mandate **requires your dev team**. This can't be actioned through the Dashboard.

**What you need to know**

- Any update triggers a new consent cycle. The mandate can't be used until your user consents to the updated details.
- Once your dev team submits the update, they'll receive a consent URL. Share this URL with your user.
- After the user consents, the mandate status returns to `Enabled`.

**What to do**

1. Collect the updated mandate details from your user (refer to the checklist in [Gather the required information](#gather-the-required-information-for-a-mandate)).
1. Pass the update request to your dev team with the **mandate ID** and the details that need changing.
1. Your dev team can follow the [Update a mandate](https://docs.swan.io/topics/payments/direct-debit/guide-update-mandate/) guide.
1. Once they've submitted the update, share the consent URL with your user.
1. If the mandate stays in `ConsentInitiationPending` for more than 24 hours, follow up with your user.

## Understand when payments are executed

Swan checks all pending direct debit instructions at **20:00 CET/CEST** on the day the payment is due. At that point, Swan checks:

1. The mandate status is `Enabled`.
1. The account's available balance is sufficient.

If both checks pass, Swan executes the payment and the transaction status updates to `Booked`. If either check fails, Swan rejects the payment.

:::tip
If your user consents to a mandate after 20:00 CET on the due date, Swan won't process it until the next scheduled payment. Let your users know about this timing so they understand why their consent might still show as pending.
:::

## Troubleshoot rejected direct debits

| Cause | How to check | What to do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| B2B mandate not consented | Mandate status is `ConsentInitiationPending` or `ConsentPending` | Initiate consent or remind your user to complete it. |
| Insufficient available balance | Check available balance (not booked) before 20:00 CET | Your user must add funds before the next execution date. |
| Account closed or closing | Check account status | A new account is needed. |
| Invalid mandate information | Creditor's details don't match registered mandate | Contact the creditor to verify UMR and SCI. |

## Stop or cancel a direct debit

To suspending or cancel a mandate, your dev team can action this, or reach out to Swan Support.

| Action | What it does | Reversible? | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Suspend** | Blocks future debits. Any pending instructions are rejected. | Yes. It can be re-enabled. | User wants to temporarily pause debits from a creditor. |
| **Cancel** | Permanently cancels the mandate | No | User wants to permanently stop all debits from a creditor. |

:::note
If your integration already supports this, your user may be able to do suspend or cancel directly from your product's interface.
:::

**What to do:**

1. Confirm with your user whether they want to suspend (temporary) or cancel (permanent).
1. Pass the request to your dev team with the **mandate ID** (visible in **Dashboard** > **Received direct debit mandates**) and the action (`suspend` or `cancel`).
1. Your dev team can follow the [Suspend a mandate](https://docs.swan.io/topics/payments/direct-debit/guide-suspend-mandate/) or [Cancel a mandate](https://docs.swan.io/topics/payments/direct-debit/guide-cancel-mandate/) guide.

**Can't reach your dev team?** Contact Swan support with the mandate ID and requested action.

## Understand refund rights

Your user's refund rights depend on their mandate scheme. For full details, including timelines for returns and creditor reversals, refer to the [SEPA Direct Debit specifications](https://docs.swan.io/topics/payments/direct-debit/#specifications).

**Quick reference for your support conversations:**

- **Core mandate**: Your user can request a refund up to **8 calendar weeks** after execution, no questions asked. Up to **13 months** if the mandate was invalid or the payment wasn't authorized.
- **B2B mandate**: **No refund possible.** Your user waived refund rights when they consented to the mandate.

If your user asks for a refund on a B2B mandate, explain that they agreed to this when they signed the mandate. If they believe the debit was unauthorized, escalate to Swan Support — this becomes a dispute, not a refund.

## Gather required mandate information

Before creating or updating a mandate, collect:

- Debtor's full name.
- Debtor's IBAN
- Debtor's country of residence
- SEPA creditor identifier (SCI)
- Unique mandate reference (UMR)
- Mandate scheme: SDD Core or SDD B2B
- Mandate sequence: recurrent or one-off
- Signature date and language

## Contact Swan Support

Contact Swan if:

- A mandate update has been submitted correctly but remains in an unexpected status for more than **48 hours**.
- A direct debit was rejected and the mandate and account status both appear correct.
- You need to investigate a rejection that doesn't match the causes listed above.

Include in your request:

- Mandate ID
- Account ID
- Expected vs. actual behavior
- Any API error messages from your dev team
