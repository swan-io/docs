### Overview

Card packages are preconfigured, tailored offers that help you launch market-ready card products faster.

When you create a card product, you select a package. 
Each package includes a predefined set of features and settings:

- **Insurance**: Each package has a set insurance level: **Standard**, **Essential**, or **Premium**. 
- **Spending limits**: Higher tiers unlock higher transaction limits.
- **Foreign exchange (FX) fees**: Higher-tier packages offer reduced or waived FX fees.
- **Card design**: Standard and Essential packages offer silver and black card designs. Only the **Premium** package offers the **metal** card. 

### Card package comparison

| Feature | Standard | Essential | Premium |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Insurance level | Standard | Essential | Premium |
| Spending limits | Base-limit | Increased | Maximum |
| Foreign exchange fees* | Base-rate | Reduced | Waived |
| Card design | Plastic: Black or Silver | Plastic: Black or Silver | Metal |

**Note: FX fees within the Premium package are waived for the user. Refer to your T&Cs for details on FX billing.* 

:::info Availability and rollout
You can select card packages for all new card products created after the feature launch (TBC). 
You can't retroactively apply insurance or specific package benefits to existing cards. 
If you want to offer a card package to existing users, create a new card product and issue new cards.
:::

### Card product configuration {#card-product-configuration}

You can assign one of the three card packages at the card product level. 
Once set, the package automatically links to newly issued cards. 
Read our step-by-step guide to learn how to [configure your card product](/topics/cards/card-packages/guide-product-configuration).

## Card insurance {#insurance}

Swan offers three insurance coverage options for cards. 
The insurance coverage included depends on the card package you select for your card product.

After you assign a card package to a card product, all newly issued cards automatically receive the associated insurance coverage. 
No further action is needed.

:::caution No coverage for existing cards
You can't upgrade existing cards with insurance. To provide coverage, issue a new card under a product with insurance.
:::

### Card package billing {#package-billing}

Billing for card packages, whether to the partner or your user, depends on your Swan contract, the selected card package, and your chosen [billing model](/topics/accounts/billing/guide-compliant-billing). Fees include:

- **Card subscription**: Monthly fee per active card.
- **FX fees**: Charged per transaction in non-euro currencies.
- **Physical cards**: One-time printing and shipping fee (billed to Partner only).

For more information, see the [usage-based billing metrics](/topics/accounts/billing/#billing-metrics).
For detailed pricing, refer to your T&Cs or submit a request to Swan Support.

### Insurance subscription {#insurance-subscription}

:::tip Prerequisites
- Account membership is `Enabled`.
- Account holder verification status is `Verified`. 

The creation of a subscription may be **delayed** until these conditions are met.
:::

When a user orders a card package that includes insurance, Swan manages the insurance subscription with our insurance partner.

The subscription progresses through the following statuses: `Pending` → `Processing` → `Enabled`, or `Canceled`.

The card status determines the insurance status. It's `Enabled` when a card is ordered and `Canceled` when the card is canceled.

:::info Card packages activation
Once subscribed, the insurance activates automatically. Your user can view the insurance notice and access the insurance claims portal URL in their Web Banking.
:::

### Card insurance claims {#insurance-claims}

Depending on the type of claim, cardholders can seek support through the Swan, our insurance claims portal, or our emergency assistance provider:

- **Fraud or phishing**: Report directly to Swan.
- **All other claims**: Submit these through the insurance claims portal.
- **Urgent assistance**: Contact our assistance provider (if included in their offer).

#### Reporting fraud and phishing {#report-fraud-phishing}

When a cardholder reports fraud or phishing to Swan, we help secure the account or card and guide them through any additional steps.

:::info Card fraud dispute article
Share the dedicated [Support Center article](https://support.swan.io/hc/en-150/articles/17099978406045-Credit-transfer-and-card-payment-fraud-disputes#h_01HQHA9YY8M234ZE7AV1GY6APF) to help your users file their fraud dispute.
:::

#### Reporting through the claims portal {#claims-portal}

In most cases, cardholders should submit the claim through the insurance claims portal, which is available through cardholder's Web Banking interface. 
It's typically used for standard claims such as: 

- Lost documents
- Travel delays
- Purchase protection
- Card fraud

To submit a claim, cardholders must:
1. Access the insurance claims portal using their **Swan account email address**. Swan automatically creates an account for the cardholder containing all their covered cards.
1. **Set a password** on first connection to complete their account setup.
1. **Upload the required documents** directly to the portal.

Our **insurance partner manages the entire claims process** after submission. 
Cardholders must follow up and track the progress of their claim directly through the portal. 
If our insurance partner approves compensation, they credit it directly to the cardholder's Swan account.

:::info Customized claims portal    
By default, our insurance partner co-brands the claims management interface with your **logo and accent color**, like your Swan interfaces (Web Banking and consent screens).
:::

#### Reporting to our assistance provider

Our assistance provider is only available with the **Premium** card package. They handle urgent claims such as:
- Medical emergencies  
- Repatriation  
- Hospitalisation  
- Legal assistance

These must be reported **by phone** for immediate support.

Our assistance provider manages the entire claims process. 
If they approve compensation, they credit it directly to the cardholder's Swan account.
