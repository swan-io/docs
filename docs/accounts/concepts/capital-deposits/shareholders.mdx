# Shareholders

A shareholder is a natural person (individual) or legal entity that deposits funds in exchange for ownership of the future company.
To deposit funds, the shareholder opens a **temporary** Swan payment account.

## Opening the temporary account {#account}

Because they're opening an account, each shareholder must go through the [onboarding](/accounts/guides/onboarding), [identification](/users/concepts/identifications), and [account holder verification](/accounts/concepts/account-holders/verification#verification-process) processes.
Shareholders must also provide proof of their residence address; only official documents are validated.
Refer to the list of acceptable documents in [proof of address](/accounts/reference/onboarding/proof-of-address).

After completing these requirements, the shareholder's account is assigned an IBAN they can use to deposit funds.

A shareholder moves through several [statuses](/accounts/concepts/capital-deposits/statuses) as they onboard and transfer funds. See [shareholder statuses](/accounts/reference/capital-deposits#shareholders-statuses).

As soon as the `CapitalDepositCase` is complete, the shareholder's temporary account **closes automatically**.

## Transfer requirements {#transfer-requirements}

:::caution Shareholder accounts only accept SEPA Credit Transfers
Capital can only be deposited into a shareholder account via [SEPA Credit Transfers](/payments/concepts/credit-transfers/sepa/).
International credit transfers are **rejected automatically** before settlement, regardless of the amount.
Ensure all transfers are sent from the shareholder's account in their name.
:::
