## New features

🧭 The new [Onboarding API](/topics/onboarding/) is now live in beta, built to strengthen risk assessment and regulatory compliance through improved data collection. It also delivers a better developer experience with cleaner mutations and queries, structured inputs, and typed responses. The documentation has been fully updated to reflect all new validations.

## Improvements

🔎 Added an optional `reason` string field to `CapitalDepositDocumentRefusedStatusInfo`, providing a free-text explanation when a capital deposit document is refused. You can now view the exact refusal explanation from your dashboard instead of relying solely on the generic `reasonCode` description.

🔍 Account holders can now be filtered by their KYC verification requirements, both in the Dashboard and via the API. Supported filters include `FirstTransferRequired`, `SupportingDocumentsRequired`, `UboDetailsRequired`, `LegalRepresentativeDetailsRequired`, `OrganizationDetailsRequired`, `TaxIdRequired`, and `Other`.

## API updates

### Reminders

⚠️ Starting **April 2**, the four [`CloseAccountReasonType`](https://api-reference.swan.io/enums/close-account-reason-type/) values used for account opening refusals, capital deposit cancellations, and retroactive account closures are replaced with more granular codes that provide better transparency about why an account was closed: `ComplianceReason`, `PartnerReason`, `Inactivity`, and `CapitalDepositReason`.

Accounts closed before **April 2** keep their original reason codes. Refer to the [deprecated reason codes](/changelog/26-february-2026/#upcoming-breaking-changes) for the full mapping.

👉 Check all reminders of already-announced upcoming breaking changes on [docs.swan.io/changelog](/changelog).

## Coming up

🏛️ In April, we're launching a new Partner-branded Re-KYC portal, enabling account administrators to complete periodic KYC reviews through an intuitive, multilingual interface with pre-filled data and automated email reminders.

💻 A new desktop sign-up flow for Web Banking is coming soon. Authentication and personal information will be completed on desktop: users receive a 6-digit OTP code by SMS, enter the code, create a passcode, then enter personal information before switching to mobile to complete identification. This keeps authentication on a single device, adding a critical layer of friction against phishing and social engineering fraud. No changes are required on your side.

👀 Starting **April 2**, Swan adds a cancelation reason code on `CapitalDepositCaseCanceledStatusInfo` when the case status is `Canceled`. You can also subscribe to the `CapitalDepositCaseEvent.Canceled` webhook to receive notifications when a capital deposit case is canceled.

Capital deposit cases canceled before **April 2** have a `null` cancelation reason at the capital deposit level; for those cases, refer to the account-level deprecated closure reason `CapitalDepositReason`.

Check out **[Swan's public roadmap](https://www.swan.io/roadmap)** to see what's in the works. 🚀
