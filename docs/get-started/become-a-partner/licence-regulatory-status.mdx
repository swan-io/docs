# Licence and regulatory status

## Swan's license {#license}

Swan holds an [e-money license](https://www.regafi.fr/spip.php?page=results&type=advanced&id_secteur=3&lang=en&denomination=Swan&siren=853827103&cib=17328&bic=&nom=&siren_agent=&num=&cat=0&retrait=0) valid across the [European Economic Area](/get-started/become-a-partner/country-coverage) (EEA) overseen by France's [Autorité de Contrôle Prudentiel et Résolution](https://acpr.banque-france.fr/en) (ACPR).
In English, ACPR translates to *Prudential Supervision and Resolution Authority*.

Because Swan holds the license, **Swan assumes responsibility for all sensitive and risky banking operations**.
While many operations can be delegated to you, some are always performed by Swan for regulatory purposes.
For example, consent is *always* required for [sensitive operations](/users/reference/sensitive-operations), and consent *always* involves Swan in a Live environment.

## Your legal status {#legal-status}

Your legal status while partnering with Swan depends on the country in which your company is based: in France, or anywhere else in the European Economic Area (EEA).

| Location | Status |
| --- | --- |
| 🇫🇷 France | You'll operate as a **Registered Swan Intermediary**.<br /><br />Learn more in the [dedicated guide for France](/get-started/become-a-partner/orias-registration). |
| All other EEA countries | You'll operate as a **Commercial Agent of Swan**.<br /><br />You don't have to complete a regulatory process to become a Commercial Agent. Swan handles it once you go Live. |

## Restricted businesses {#restricted-businesses}

Swan builds infrastructure for businesses to embed financial services. 
Most types of companies can embed banking services with Swan, but some aren't allowed. 
Swan must also follow all local laws and rules from banks, payment networks, and regulators.

This decision is risk-based, not preference-based. 
Swan won't work with industries that could harm the reputation or finances of Swan or its partners.

Restricted activities are categorized into two groups:

1. **Common restricted activities**: This list applies to all Swan Partners. Your users can access this list in our dedicated [Support Center article](https://support.swan.io/hc/en-150/articles/5767255782045-Restricted-businesses).  
2. **Additional restricted activities**: These apply only to Partners with specific regulated services, such as Neobanks, Accounts Payable, and Capital deposits. Please contact your PIM (Partner Integration Manager) to confirm if these apply to your service.

Businesses engaged in activities listed in either group will be ineligible to open an account.

### Common restricted activities

Swan restricts activities in the following list for all users:

| **Category** | **Restricted activities** |
| --- | --- |
| Financial and regulatory risk activities | <ul><li>Shell banks</li><li>Trust and offshore company service providers</li><li>Company domiciliation business</li><li>Non-licensed money service businesses</li><li>Non-licensed payment service providers</li></ul> |
| Crypto-assets and virtual currencies | <ul><li>Crypto-assets trading / exchange platforms</li><li>Crypto-assets custody platforms</li><li>Crypto-assets mixing activity</li><li>Crypto ATM</li></ul> |
| Controversial or sensitive activities | <ul><li>Prostitution / escort</li><li>Organisation known as sects</li><li>Adult entertainment</li></ul> |
| Substance and materials-related activities | <ul><li>Marijuana culture, products and trading</li><li>Scrap metal dealers</li><li>Weapons and defense materials providers</li></ul> |
| High-risk or unregulated commerce | <ul><li>Door-to-door sales</li><li>Taxi phone and call shops</li></ul> |
