Company account onboarding

Company account onboarding

Process of creating a new company account holder, and opening a new Swan account for that account holder.

Self-employed users are sometimes included in the company group, depending on the account country. Refer to the country requirements section for more information about accounts for self-employed users.

Process overviewâ€‹

Your main action is to create a company onboarding link. The link leads your user to a five-step form through which Swan collects essential information to open the account.

When creating an onboarding link for your users, you can pre-fill most of the information for them. When your user goes through the form, they'll confirm that the information you entered is correct. Users can also fill in this information themselves.

User flow diagram

To understand more about your user's onboarding experience, refer to the user flow diagram for company account onboarding.

Company onboarding formâ€‹

The company onboarding form begins by gathering preliminary details about the company. Then, Swan collects the following information:

  1. User's email address.
  2. Legal information about the organization, including the company name, registration number (optional), VAT number (optional), and address, as well as details about the organization's activity and expected monthly volume.
  3. Information about the company's ultimate beneficial owners (UBO). This step only applies if the user selected company, association (specifically home owner association), or other when submitting their preliminary details.
  4. Required documents to verify your organization.
    • During onboarding, Swan completes an identity analysis (Know Your Business, or KYB).
    • For the lifetime of the account, Swan monitors for fraud.
  5. Consent to open the account (consent process requires a mobile device).

For German company accounts, Swan also collects the following during steps 1-3:

  1. Legal representative's country of residence and residence address.
  2. Company tax number (Umsatzsteuer-Identifikationsnummer).
  3. UBO's country of residence, residence address, and, if country of residence is DEU, UBO's tax identification number (Steuer-Identifikationsnummer).

Identify verification recommendationsâ€‹

Swan must verify the identity of every company. Swan supports multiple types of identity verification, called identificationLevels in the API.

For company onboarding, the following levels are recommended based on the account country. Please refer to the section on identity verification for more information on identification levels.

Requirements vary country to country when opening any type of Swan account. Requirements for company accounts are different than for individual accounts, and there is also a different mutation.

For company accounts, use the onboardCompanyAccountHolder mutation to collect the following information according to your desired account country.
